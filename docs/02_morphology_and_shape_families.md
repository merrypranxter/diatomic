# Morphology and shape families

Classical diatom morphology divides forms into **centric** and **pennate** based largely on symmetry and outline.
This repo overlays a more visual "shape family" taxonomy on top of that to organize the catalog.

---

## Centric vs pennate

- **Centric diatoms**
  - Radial or rotational symmetry in valve view.
  - Outlines: discs, polygons, sometimes elongate cylinders in girdle view.
  - Ornamentation arranged radially or in concentric patterns.

- **Pennate diatoms**
  - Bilateral symmetry along a long axis in valve view (sometimes with asymmetry about a second axis).
  - Outlines: naviculoid boats, cymbelloid bananas, gomphonemoid clubs, sigmoids, ribbons.
  - Ornamentation organized along an apical axis, often with a raphe system.

---

## Shape families used here

These "shape families" are deliberately visual and loose — they capture what a form *looks* like, not necessarily its phylogenetic affinity.

### Centric families

| Shape family | Outline | Key feature | Example genera |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disc radiate | Near-circular | Radial striae from centre | *Coscinodiscus*, *Actinoptychus* |
| Polygonal & stellate | Triangular, hexagonal, starburst | Defined corners or arms | *Triceratium*, *Asteromphalus* |
| Cylindrical chain formers | Cylinder in girdle view | Linking structures at valve margins | *Melosira*, *Aulacoseira* |

### Pennate families

| Shape family | Outline | Key feature | Example genera |
|---|---|---|---|
| Naviculoid boats | Elongated, pointed ends | Central raphe, bilateral symmetry | *Navicula*, *Pinnularia* |
| Cymbelloid bananas | Curved, dorsal/ventral sides | Offset raphe, dorsiventral asymmetry | *Cymbella*, *Encyonema* |
| Gomphonemoid clubs | Asymmetric, broadened at one end | Stalk/mucilage pad end | *Gomphonema*, *Gomphoneis* |
| Sigmoid and wavy | S-shaped or sinuous axis | Curved long axis | *Gyrosigma*, *Pleurosigma* |
| Ribbon and filamentous | Long chains in girdle view | Linked valves forming ribbons | *Fragilaria*, *Diatoma* |

---

## Why use shape families instead of taxonomy?

Shape families are easier to connect to:

- **Mathematical descriptions** – it is more natural to say "parameterize a sigmoid centerline" than "parameterize *Gyrosigma*".
- **Visual searches** – when looking at a microscope image, you first see shape, then details.
- **Generative models** – shaders and parametric models target geometric forms, not taxonomic units.

Each catalog page cross-references any formal taxonomic context it is aware of, but the primary organizing principle is geometry.
